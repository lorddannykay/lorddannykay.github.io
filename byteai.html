Product architecture for AI-enabled education — memory-aware tutoring, adaptive sequencing, and LMS integration without platform rip-and-replace. Built open-source so teams can audit, self-host, and extend.

Author

Sudar Studio

From a document or URL to a published course in minutes — AI-assisted authoring, templates, and org governance in one creator surface.

• AI course generation from documents and URLs
• SCORM 1.2 import and export
• Learning paths, assignments, and analytics
• Org-wide governance and approval flows

Deliver

Sudar Learn

Every learner gets a tutor, a path, and seven ways to learn — powered by a persistent Digital Learner Twin that updates as people actually learn.

• Seven learning modalities (read, listen, watch, map, cards, feed, play)
• Tutor Sudar with cross-session memory and proactive nudges
• Next-best-action recommendations
• Progress tracking, paths, and certificates

Adapt

Sudar Intelligence (ALP)

The Adaptive Learning Layer — reference HTTP APIs so Moodle, Canvas, and other LMSs gain learner memory and memory-aware tutoring without full platform replacement.

• Digital Learner Twin and adaptive sequencing
• ALP plugin architecture for existing LMS stacks
• Tutor and next-best-action services
• Open-weight inference — ~$0.021/learner/month marginal AI cost

Create

ByteVerse

A free, open-source AI microlearning factory. Turn any source material into SCORM-ready microlearning in minutes — bring your own LLM or use included free models, with no vendor lock-in.

• Upload PDFs, URLs, and docs — export SCORM 1.2 or HTML
• Pedagogy-first modules with ADDIE and Bloom-tagged objectives
• Bring your own keys (OpenAI, Groq, Ollama, and more)
• Pairs with Sudar Learn for adaptive delivery

For Investors & Partners

Why Open-Source AI for Education

EdTech's next moat is not another LMS — it's learner intelligence that compounds over time. Sudar encodes that in a Digital Learner Twin; ALP productises it as plugins into Moodle, Canvas, and enterprise stacks. ByteVerse attacks the content bottleneck that slows every rollout.

Unit economics: Open-weight inference ~$0.021/learner/month (ALP benchmark).
Distribution: Apache-2.0 · self-host · community extension · enterprise services layer.
Defensibility: Memory-aware tutoring research (LAMP 2026) · multimodal delivery · integrated author-learn-adapt stack.
